⚠️ The Problem: Quantum Threat to VPNs

⚛️

Shor's Algorithm

A quantum computer can break RSA-2048 and ECC in polynomial time — rendering classical VPN key exchange obsolete.

🗄️

Harvest-Now, Decrypt-Later

Adversaries record encrypted traffic today and decrypt it once quantum hardware matures — your data is at risk now.

🔑

VPNs are Prime Targets

VPNs continuously transmit sensitive organizational data — making them the highest-priority target for harvest attacks.

📅

NIST Responds

In 2024, NIST released FIPS 203 (ML-KEM) — the first standardized post-quantum key encapsulation mechanism.

✅ Our Solution: PQ-VPN Tunnel

🔐

Kyber512 Key Encapsulation

ML-KEM (NIST FIPS 203) — lattice-based, secure against both classical and quantum adversaries. Replaces RSA in the handshake.

🔒

AES-256-GCM Encryption

All tunnel data is encrypted with authenticated AEAD encryption. 12-byte nonce + 16-byte tag = 28-byte constant overhead.

🌐

Real Layer-3 Tunnel

Linux TUN virtual interfaces over UDP. Two physical machines on a university Wi-Fi — real-world deployment, not a simulation.

📊

Quantified Performance

Full benchmark suite comparing Kyber512 vs RSA-2048: handshake time, CPU load, packet overhead, and throughput.

The critical distinction: both systems encrypt data with AES-256-GCM — but only our system protects the key establishment from quantum adversaries. MLWE hardness provides security equivalent to AES-128 against both classical and quantum attacks.
